# Elves & Men — Canonical Lore → Skill-Tree Abilities

**Scope:** Playable cultures in the `middle-earth` Fabric 1.21.8 mod: **Elves** (split by faction into **Lothlórien / Galadhrim** and **Woodland Realm / Silvan**), and **Men** — **Gondor** (Dúnedain), **Rohan** (Rohirrim), **Dale** (Lake-men/archers). For each: canonical traits with citations → concrete gameplay ability → lore-flavored tradeoff. Ends with a skill-tree branch sketch per culture.

**Design conventions used throughout**
- **[Passive]** = always-on attribute modifier / status-effect application, no keybind.
- **[Active]** = keybind-triggered ability, usually with a cooldown and/or resource cost (hunger, saturation, a held item, health, or a charge-up).
- Attribute names in `code font` refer either to vanilla attributes (`minecraft:generic.*`) or plausible custom `middle-earth:*` attributes, matching the pattern already established by the mod (`powdered_snow_immunity`, `climbing_strength`, `detection_range`, `delvers_fear_strength`).
- Citations use short-form (Book, Chapter) for primary Tolkien text, plus the specific secondary source/quote found via research where a primary edition wasn't directly searchable. All sources are listed at the end of each culture section.

## 1. ELVES (Lothlórien / Galadhrim & Woodland Realm / Silvan)

Both Elven factions in the mod are drawn from the same underlying race (the mod's `Race` object is faction-agnostic — see the base study), so the bulk of "what it means to be an Elf" is **shared canon** that should live in a common Elf trunk. The two factions diverge in *flavor* and get their own branch/capstone: Lothlórien = Noldor/Sindar-ruled **Galadhrim**, high, numinous, Ring-adjacent, foresight and light; Woodland Realm = **Silvan** wood-elves under Thranduil, earthier, warier, wood-and-feast-hall craft, less touched by the Elder Days but no less deadly in their own forest.

### 1.1 Shared canonical traits → abilities

| # | Canonical trait & citation | Gameplay ability | Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Elves rest without truly "sleeping" as Men do — eyes open, resting the mind while the body stays alert.** *"Legolas already lay motionless, his fair hands folded upon his breast, his eyes unclosed, blending living night and deep dream, as is the way with Elves."* (*The Two Towers*, "The White Rider"); Legolas "could sleep, if sleep it could be called by Men... even as he walked open-eyed in the light of this world." | **[Passive] Waking Rest** — sleeping in a bed (or even standing still) grants the sleep/bed-spawnpoint benefit and clears the `Sleep Deprivation`/phantom-spawn timer without the screen going black or the player losing awareness; while "resting," the Elf still gets a small `regeneration` tick and retains a reduced FOV/limited-input state rather than full unconsciousness (can be interrupted instantly with no groggy debuff). Elves never get the vanilla Phantom "insomnia" bad-luck stacking. | Elves get **no** the vanilla comfort/warmth cuddling exploits some servers rely on (no "skip the night" AFK farming shortcut beyond normal) — flavor-only tradeoff; mechanically this is a pure QoL upside, so pair it with the melancholy debuff below to keep it balanced. |
| 2 | **Elves are tireless — they run/travel for days without exhaustion.** *"On and on he led them, tireless and swift... it was Aragorn who called a halt."* (*The Two Towers*, "The Departure of Boromir" / the chase of the Three Hunters) — Legolas outlasts Aragorn and Gimli entirely. | **[Passive] Tireless Stride** — immune to the Exhaustion/`hunger` drain from sprinting and jumping; no Mining Fatigue from starvation; sprint never depletes saturation below a floor. Functions as a `generic.sprint_efficiency`-style zero-cost sprint. | Elves get **no bonus regeneration from food** beyond satiety — lembas aside (see below), a starving Elf still takes the same Weakness at 0 hunger as anyone; their tirelessness is stamina, not metabolism. |
| 3 | **Elves are immune to disease and the natural cold; they do not sicken.** (General Elvish-lore consensus: Elves' *hröar* (bodies) do not degrade or fall ill the way mortal bodies do, per their nature as an "immortal spirit bound to an undying-while-Arda-lasts body" — Silmarillion "Of the Beginning of Days" framing of Elvish fëa/hröa union.) | **[Passive] Deathless Flesh** — immune to the `Poison`/withering from Powdered Snow already present in the base mod (kept), extended to full **Freezing immunity** (never accrues freeze ticks in snowy biomes/powder snow/water in cold biomes) and immunity to any custom "plague"/disease-style debuffs the mod might add later. | No frost-resistance *combat* bonus — an Elf still takes full damage from cold-based attacks (Frost effects, ice weapons); this is only survival-immunity, not damage reduction. |
| 4 | **Legolas walks on snow without sinking — Elves are light-footed almost to weightlessness.** *"For running light over grass and leaf, or over snow — an Elf."* ... Frodo notices "the Elf had no boots, but wore only light shoes... and his feet made little imprint in the snow." (*The Fellowship of the Ring*, "The Ring Goes South.") | **[Passive] Snow-Walker** — no movement-speed penalty on Snow/Snow Layers/Powder Snow/Soul Sand/Honey (the mod already has a `move_efficiency +0.3` and powdered-snow immunity for Elf — this extends it explicitly to full "never slowed by terrain" on snow & soul sand); leaves no footprints/doesn't trigger pressure plates or trip wire at walk speed. | Because they're so light, Elves take **+10% knockback** from explosions/blows (less mass to resist force) — a real, minor combat tradeoff. |
| 5 | **Elven eyesight is famed — spotting riders, colors, and count at leagues' distance.** *"Keen are the eyes of the Elves"* (Aragorn, *The Two Towers*); Legolas counts "one hundred and five" riders and reads hair/spear colors at a distance Aragorn sees only as a blur; Gandalf: Elves "can tell a sparrow from a finch a league off." | **[Passive] Eyes of the Eldar** — passive Night Vision; entities within a wide radius (32–48 blocks) that are behind foliage/fog get a subtle Glowing-style outline (implemented as client-side highlighting, not full Glowing so it doesn't reveal to others); render distance for entities effectively extended (mobs that would normally cull at distance still tick/show). | **Elves take psychic strain from extreme close, dark confinement** — see the "Barrow-fear"/underground debuff below; their eyes are built for starlight and horizon, not black tunnels. |
| 6 | **Elven hearing is preternatural** (Legolas hears things Men and Dwarves miss throughout the journey; Gimli notes only Legolas/Aragorn catch faint sounds). | **[Passive] Keen Ear** — subtitles/sound-source markers for mob footsteps and hostile-mob spawns within range appear earlier than vanilla; hostile mobs that would sneak up (Creepers, Endermen teleport-in) give an early warning cue (a faint audio/HUD ping) before they're in strike range. | None mechanically new here beyond #5's tradeoff — bundled as flavor with Eyes of the Eldar. |
| 7 | **Elves are unmatched archers and swordsmen** — Legolas' entire combat role in the Fellowship is ranged precision + blade-work; broader Elvish martial reputation runs through the Silmarillion (Fëanorians, Fingon, etc.). | **[Passive/Active] Elven Marksman** — bow draw-to-full-power time reduced (~25% faster full charge); arrows fired at full draw gain reduced gravity drop and a small guaranteed-crit chance on longshots (>15 blocks). Sword hits have a small chance to not consume durability ("keen edge"). | Elven weapons are light and precise, **not brutal** — melee `attack_damage` gets no bonus (matches the base study: Elf is 1.0 attack, same as Human) and heavy weapons (axes, maces) get a small draw-speed *penalty* for Elves (unfamiliar heft). |
| 8 | **Elven herb-lore and healing** (subtler than the Númenórean "hands of the king," but Elves are the source of *athelas'* fame and Elrond is the greatest healer in Middle-earth — *"few could rival Elrond in the arts of healing."*) | **[Passive] Herb-Lore** — Elves get bonus effect duration/potency from potions brewed with Nether Wart-tier ingredients and from natural regen items (golden apples, honey); harvesting flowers restores a small amount of saturation directly (foraged sustenance). | Elves have no combat-healing burst equivalent to Gondor's capstone (see Men) — their healing is slow-and-steady, not a channel-heal-allies active; that's deliberately reserved as the Dúnedain's signature so the two cultures stay differentiated. |
| 9 | **Elves resist (but are not wholly immune to) the domination of the Ring / Sauron's will** — Galadriel is offered the Ring and *passes the test*, refusing it and choosing to diminish rather than fall (*The Fellowship of the Ring*, "The Mirror of Galadriel": *"I pass the test... I will diminish, and go into the West, and remain Galadriel."*). Elves as a kindred are depicted as harder to cow with fear/despair than Men (never fully immune — Sauron still terrifies armies of Elves at points in the Second Age). | **[Passive] Unbowed Will** — increased resistance duration/chance against `Nausea`/`Darkness`/other "dread" status effects the mod might apply for boss auras or evil-faction abilities; reduced potency of any future "Ring-corruption" mechanic scaling on this culture specifically. | Deliberately **not** full immunity — keep a residual chance for dread effects to land, and Elves get **no bonus vs. direct charm/mind-control from Ring-bearers** (thematically, an Elf who actually *takes up* the Ring is arguably *more* dangerous to the world, not less — a hook for future "corrupted elf" content, not a buff). |
| 10 | **Elven-cloaks: masterwork camouflage, not invisibility** — *"grey in twilight but green when moved, or brown as fields, dusk-silver in the night... hide the wearers... light, and warm or cool as needed."* (*The Fellowship of the Ring*, "Farewell to Lórien.") | **[Passive] Elven-Cloak Craft** (or a craftable Elf-only cloak item) — while sneaking, Elves get a stronger `detection_range` reduction than the Hobbit's stealth stat when standing in foliage/leaf blocks specifically (thematically "blends with the wood"); mobs' line-of-sight detection radius shrinks further for an Elf in a forest/jungle biome. | The bonus is **biome-gated** — in open plains/deserts/stone the Elf gets no special stealth (unlike the Hobbit's flat sneak-speed bonus which works everywhere); Elves are creatures of green places. |
| 11 | **Elven-rope**: exceptional cordage that unties itself at the wielder's will, from mallorn-bark fiber (*The Fellowship of the Ring*, "Farewell to Lórien" — Sam's gift). | **[Active] Elven Rope** (item/ability) — a grapple-like rope/hook usable a limited number of times per day that lets an Elf ascend or descend a short vertical distance instantly (fall-damage-negating), thematically "climbing gear," distinct from the Goblin's wall-cling. | Limited charges (recharges slowly, e.g. once per Minecraft day) — it's a masterwork gift-item, not infinite mobility. |
| 12 | **Lembas — waybread that sustains far beyond its size, but repeated reliance makes mortals "weary of their mortality... longing for fields... to which they cannot come."** (*The Fellowship of the Ring* / *The Two Towers*, "Of Herbs and Stewed Rabbit.") | **[Passive/Item]** Elf-crafted lembas (a craftable food unique to the Elf culture) provides very high saturation-per-hunger-point efficiency for an Elf eating it — but for a Man or other mortal eating it repeatedly, apply a stacking, harmless-but-flavorful `Sea-Longing`-style debuff (see Rohan/Gondor/Dale sections) — a cross-culture design hook that rewards *trading* lembas to Men sparingly rather than as a staple. | For the Elf themself: no drawback, it's their own bread. |
| 13 | **The "Sea-longing"** — a melancholy that, once woken (typically by the cry of gulls), never fully leaves an Elf and eventually draws them West. Legolas is warned by Galadriel: *"Beware of the Sea! If thou hearest the cry of the gull on the shore, Thy heart shall then rest in the forest no more."* He hears the gulls at Pelargir and is marked from then on (*The Return of the King*, "The Last Debate" / "Many Partings"). | **[Passive/Debuff — flavor mechanic] Sea-Longing** — an optional slow-burn debuff track: an Elf player who spends long stretches away from ocean/coast biomes (or specifically near a "Gulls of Pelargir"-style trigger) accrues stacks of a mild `Nausea`/unfocused-effect that clears when they visit open water/coast, modeling restlessness inland. Alternatively (lighter-touch): a cosmetic-only mood system with no hard mechanical penalty, just ambient sound/particle flavor. | This is intentionally the Elves' signature **long-game debuff** — immortality is not free; it is a slow pull away from Middle-earth. Recommend implementing as a soft, avoidable flavor mechanic rather than a hard DPS/speed penalty, so it doesn't feel punishing. |
| 14 | **Elves are diminished/uneasy underground, away from starlight and green growing things** (thematically consistent with their origin "under the stars" at Cuiviénen and their discomfort in Moria compared to Gimli's joy there — *"the Dwarves tell no tale... but this is not a Dwarf-country"* framing throughout *The Fellowship of the Ring*, "A Journey in the Dark"; contrast Legolas' unease vs. Gimli's wonder in Moria). | **[Passive] Deep-Dark Unease** (mirrors the mod's already-present but *unused* `delvers_fear_strength` attribute — a perfect existing hook!) — set a nonzero `delvers_fear_strength` for Elves specifically when below a Y-threshold with no sky access for a sustained period: a mild `Mining Fatigue`/`Slowness` or reduced `Eyes of the Eldar` radius kicks in deep underground, lifting immediately on reaching open sky or strong torchlight/starlight. | This is the direct inverse of trait #5 — the same eyes that see leagues under open sky are ill-suited to lightless stone. Gives Elves a real reason to avoid being the party's sole miner, complementing Dwarf's tankiness-underground identity. |

### 1.2 Lothlórien (Galadhrim) — sub-culture flavor

Ruled by Galadriel and Celeborn, keepers of Nenya (a Ring of Power) and the light of the Two Trees preserved in Galadriel's phial. Lothlórien is explicitly the most numinous, time-frozen, foresight-touched Elven realm in the story.

- **Galadriel's Mirror / foresight** — *"Many things I can command the Mirror to reveal... and to some I have shown what they desired to see."* (*FotR*, "The Mirror of Galadriel.") → **[Active] Mirror of Galadriel**: once per set cooldown (e.g. daily), a Galadhrim player can channel at a water source to reveal a hint (structure compass direction, nearby hostile-faction patrol, or a glimpse of a boss's location) — a lore-perfect "scrying" utility skill, deliberately vague/unreliable per the book's warning that visions can mislead.
- **The Phial of Galadriel** ("Light of Eärendil... a light when all other lights go out" — *FotR*, "Farewell to Lórien") → capstone, see below.
- **Lothlórien's changelessness / time distortion** (the Fellowship loses track of time in Lórien — *"the Company... could scarcely tell how many days had passed"*) → a passive minor `Mining Fatigue`-immunity/`Saturation` regen boost while standing on Mallorn-wood or in the mod's Lothlórien biome, reinforcing "home turf" bonuses.

### 1.3 Woodland Realm (Silvan) — sub-culture flavor

Thranduil's halls: a Silvan people, wearier of the wider world's politics, masters of woodcraft, feast, and a fiercely-guarded hidden path through Mirkwood (the "Elf-path," rerouted north away from Dol Guldur).

- **The hidden Elf-path & guarded borders** (rerouted away from Dol Guldur for safety) → **[Passive] Warder of the Wood**: faster movement through Woodland Realm's own claimed biome/forest tiles, plus improved detection of intruders (hostile mobs highlighted) while within it — territorial home-turf vigilance rather than Lórien's serene foresight.
- **The butts of wine and feasting-halls, and the guards' fondness for drink that famously let Bilbo's dwarves escape** (*The Hobbit*, "Barrels Out of Bond") → a light, comic trade-off node: Woodland Elves get a `Saturation`/morale buff from feast food (cooked meat + fruit) shared with allies nearby, but are also flavorfully vulnerable to a `Nausea`-on-overdrink joke-debuff if the mod ever adds alcohol items — a nod to the guards falling asleep on duty.
- **Woodcraft & barrel-lore** → crafting/utility perk: cheaper/faster boat and barrel crafting, minor swim speed bonus (rivers of Mirkwood/Esgaroth trade route flavor, also ties them thematically to Dale's Lake-men neighbors).

### 1.4 Elf skill-tree branch sketch

**Trunk (shared, unlocked by picking Elf race) → then split into Lothlórien / Woodland Realm branches once the player's faction is chosen.**

**Tier 1 — Elf-kind (passive, always on)**
1. *Waking Rest* — sleep with eyes open; no Phantom insomnia penalty.
2. *Tireless Stride* — no sprint exhaustion / hunger drain from sprint-jump.
3. *Snow-Walker* — no slowdown on snow, ice, soul sand, powder snow; powder-snow immune.
4. *Eyes of the Eldar* — passive night vision + faint outline on distant entities (32 blocks).

**Tier 2 — Wood and Blade (passive, req. any 2 of Tier 1)**
5. *Keen Ear* — early audio warning of nearby hostile spawns/sneaking mobs.
6. *Elven Marksman* — faster bow full-draw, flatter arrow arc, small longshot crit chance.
7. *Elven-Cloak Craft* — strong stealth bonus specifically in forest/leaf cover.
8. *Herb-Lore* — bonus potion duration/potency; flowers grant minor direct saturation.

**Tier 3 — Gifts of the Wood (mixed, req. Tier 2 node from same theme)**
9. *[Active] Elven Rope* — limited-use grapple/climb, negates fall damage on use.
10. *Unbowed Will* — resist dread/fear-style debuffs harder & shorter.
11. *[Item unlock] Waybread* — craft lembas (high-efficiency ration; strong debuff if abused by non-Elves, a trade good).
12. *Deep-Dark Unease* (drawback node, auto-granted, not a "choice") — mild penalty deep underground away from sky, offset by all the above.

**Tier 4 — Branch split**
- **Lothlórien path:** *Mirror of Galadriel* [Active scrying utility] → *Nenya's Peace* [passive: faster health/hunger regen while on Lothlórien home soil] → capstone.
- **Woodland Realm path:** *Warder of the Wood* [passive territorial vigilance + speed in home forest] → *Feast-Hall Fellowship* [passive: food buffs extend to nearby allied players] → capstone.

**Capstones (Tier 5, one pick)**
- **"Light of the Eldar" (Lothlórien capstone)** — modeled on the **Phial of Galadriel** (*"may it be a light to you in dark places, when all other lights go out"* — *FotR*, "Farewell to Lórien"). **[Active]**: consumes a charge to emit a burst of true light that clears Darkness-type effects from self and nearby allies, blinds/staggers nearby Evil-faction NPCs briefly (echoing its canonical effect repelling Shelob and the Watcher), and grants a short Glowing-resistance-piercing light radius even in the deepest dark (bypassing the Deep-Dark Unease debuff for its duration).
- **"Hollowed Halls no More" / "Song of Thranduil" (Woodland Realm capstone)** — modeled on Thranduil's woodland magic and the Elves' use of **song/enchantment** to disorient intruders (the dwarves' capture, the drugged wine/sleep motif, and Elvish "spell-songs" scattered through the legendarium, e.g. Lúthien's song of power as the archetype of Elvish song-magic). **[Active]**: an area pulse centered on the Elf that applies Slowness+Mining Fatigue (a "binding song") to hostile mobs in range while granting nearby allies Speed+Regeneration — a battlefield control ultimate distinct from Lothlórien's more defensive/utility capstone.

## 2. GONDOR (Dúnedain of the South-kingdom)

### 2.1 Canonical traits → abilities

| # | Canonical trait & citation | Gameplay ability | Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Númenórean-descended longevity** — the Dúnedain of Gondor originally lived thrice the span of lesser Men, waning over the Third Age; by the War of the Ring "none in Gondor had lived past 100 years" since Belecthor II, yet houses of pure blood (Aragorn's) still lived far longer — Aragorn himself reached **210 years**, longest of his line since King Arvegil (*LotR*, Appendix A). | **[Passive] Long-Years of Númenor** — Gondorians resist age-related mechanics the mod might add (e.g. immune to any future "old age" debuff systems) and get a small passive `max_health` or `Regeneration`-tier innate resilience representing tougher, longer-lived stock — mechanically modest since most Gondorians in-story are *not* Aragorn-tier long-lived, just hardier than baseline Men. | Unlike Elves, this is a **fading** gift — flavor it as the *weakest* of the "long-life" traits among playable cultures (deliberately not matching Elf immortality), reinforcing that ordinary Gondorian soldiers are mortal men doing a mortal's job. |
| 2 | **"The hands of the king are the hands of a healer, and so shall the rightful king be known"** — Ioreth's proverb, fulfilled when Aragorn uses athelas (kingsfoil) to heal Faramir, Éowyn, and Merry from the Black Breath and near-death wounds in the Houses of Healing (*The Return of the King*, "The Houses of Healing"). | **[Active] Hands of the King** — channel ability (hold still for ~2–3 seconds, consumes a crushed-athelas/kingsfoil item) that heals and cures negative status effects (Poison, Wither, a custom "Black Breath"/despair debuff) on the caster and nearby allies in a small radius. High-impact, support-defining capstone-tier active. | Requires a consumable reagent (athelas must be gathered/crafted — a mundane flower until "used by the hands of a king") and a hold-still channel that leaves the caster vulnerable — cannot be used while taking damage/mid-combat-dodge, echoing the calm, ritual nature of the scene. |
| 3 | **Aragorn's leadership steadies fear in his men, most famously at the Black Gate**, rallying Men of Gondor and Rohan against overwhelming odds and the despair-inducing presence of the Nazgûl (*The Return of the King*, "The Black Gate Opens"; the rallying-speech framing is most iconic in Peter Jackson's film adaptation — the book's equivalent is the captains' resolve and Aragorn's steadying presence before the parley with the Mouth of Sauron). | **[Active] Rally of the White Tree** — banner/horn-style AoE buff: plant or raise a banner (thematically the White Tree standard) to grant nearby allied players temporary fear/`Darkness`-effect immunity and a `Strength`/`Resistance` buff for a short duration — a leadership cooldown ability, usable in a party/raid context. | Only affects **other players/allied NPCs in radius** — no self-only spam version; must be used proactively before/during a fight, not reactively mid-solo-duel, modeling Aragorn's role as a rallying captain rather than a lone berserker. |
| 4 | **Gondor's soldier-culture: valor, discipline, heavy armor, and the Tower Guard/Citadel Guard's iconic gear** (Minas Tirith's ranked garrison — Anduril, the Tower of Guard, the White Tree banner; general martial framing across Appendix A and *RotK*). | **[Passive] Citadel Discipline** — reduced knockback taken while holding a shield; small `armor_toughness` bonus representing superior Gondorian smithing/discipline in formation combat. | Heavier armor culture trades mobility — small `movement_speed` penalty while wearing full heavy armor sets (a "steady wall, not a skirmisher" identity), contrasting with Rohan's light cavalry speed. |
| 5 | **The Stewardship / duty and honor code — the weight of responsibility** (Denethor's tragic arc as a cautionary counter-example; Boromir's fall to temptation and subsequent redemption both dramatize that Gondor's ethic is measured by *how* power and duty are borne, not just held). | **[Passive/conditional debuff] Test of the Steward** — a flavor mechanic: acting dishonorably (e.g., attacking allied-faction players/NPCs, looting from allies, or other "dishonorable" flagged actions the mod's diplomacy system could track) applies a temporary `Weakness`/reduced-banner-effectiveness debuff — Gondor's strength is explicitly conditional on integrity, unlike a race with no such social gate. | This is the "Men have no free lunch" baseline penalty made concrete for Gondor specifically: their bonuses are earned/behavioral, not innate like an Elf's senses. |
| 6 | **Dúnedain Rangers' wilderness fieldcraft** — Aragorn-as-Strider's tracking, foraging, and herb-lore honed over decades in the wild ("There is food in the wild... berry, root and herb; and I have some skill as a hunter at need.") | **[Passive] Ranger's Craft** — bonus foraging yield (higher chance of extra drops from berry bushes/crops), a mild tracking utility (footprint/particle trail on recently-passed hostile mobs), representing the Ranger heritage underlying the Kings of Gondor. | Available at a mid-tier (it's Aragorn's *pre-king* skillset) — framed as a "Ranger of the North" node bridging Gondor's dynastic backstory, not a base-tier freebie. |

### 2.2 Gondor skill-tree branch sketch

**Tier 1 — Sons of Gondor (passive)**
1. *Long-Years of Númenor* — minor passive resilience/health regen tick.
2. *Citadel Discipline* — reduced knockback with shield raised; +armor toughness.
3. *Steady the Line* — small resistance to Slowness/Weakness while standing near 2+ allies (formation fighting).

**Tier 2 — Duty and Valor (passive, req. 2 of Tier 1)**
4. *Ranger's Craft* — foraging bonus + hostile-mob tracking trail.
5. *Tower Guard's Resolve* — reduced fear/Darkness-effect duration.
6. *Test of the Steward* (auto-granted conditional debuff, ties into faction/diplomacy honor).

**Tier 3 — Banners and Herb-lore (mixed)**
7. *[Active] Rally of the White Tree* — banner buff for nearby allies (fear immunity + Strength).
8. *Kingsfoil-Lore* — passive: athelas/potions healed effects last longer when self-applied.
9. *Steward's Vigilance* — passive detection range bonus vs. Evil-faction NPCs approaching Gondor spawns (border-watch flavor).

**Tier 4 — The Return of the King (mixed, req. Tier 3)**
10. *[Active] Hands of the King* — channel heal + cure debuffs on self + nearby allies (requires crushed athelas reagent).
11. *Heir of Isildur's Bearing* — passive: bonus effectiveness of Rally of the White Tree (larger radius/longer duration).

**Capstone (Tier 5)**
- **"Elendil's Heir" / "The King Has Come"** — modeled on Aragorn's full unveiling of kingship (the Paths of the Dead's Oathbreakers answering the rightful heir's call in *RotK*, "The Passing of the Grey Company," and the Houses of Healing scene). **[Active, long cooldown]**: a battlefield-turning ultimate — banner + horn together: all nearby allies get simultaneous fear immunity, a Strength/Resistance buff, *and* a one-time burst heal (mini "Hands of the King" pulse), while nearby Evil-faction NPCs get a Weakness/Slowness "awe of the returned king" debuff. Thematically the single most powerful support-capstone in the Men roster, gated behind full commitment to the Gondor tree.

## 3. ROHAN (Rohirrim)

### 3.1 Canonical traits → abilities

| # | Canonical trait & citation | Gameplay ability | Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Rohirrim are Middle-earth's greatest horse-breeders and riders** — "the breeding and riding of horses were arts developed to their greatest height, among Men, by the Rohirrim." Their culture and economy are built entirely around horses; they even name themselves the "Horse-lords." | **[Passive] Horse-Lord** — while mounted (on any tamed horse), the mount gains bonus `movement_speed` and `jump_strength`, reduced fall damage taken, and faster acceleration from standstill; the rider also gets improved attack accuracy/no penalty for attacking while moving on horseback (vanilla MC normally doesn't penalize this, so frame as bonus reach/attack speed while mounted instead). | Rohirrim get **no bonus at all on foot** — their base (unmounted) movement speed and combat stats should be plain/baseline, since their entire identity is *predicated on the horse*; a dismounted Rider of Rohan is just a Man. |
| 2 | **The Mearas** — a unique, near-mythical breed of horses "brought by the hunter Béma (Oromë) from the West," the noblest and fastest horses in Arda, descending from Felaróf (Tolkien Gateway, "Mearas"; Appendix A, "The House of Eorl"). | **[Passive/unlock] Meara-Kinship** — a Rohan-exclusive high-tier perk: taming/riding an especially rare in-game horse variant (or any horse, flavored as "of Mearas blood") grants a stacking bonus beyond the standard Horse-Lord perk — extra speed ceiling, cannot be spooked/panicked by nearby hostile mobs (no rearing off nervously), cannot be permanently tamed by non-Rohan players (Mearas famously bear no rider but their chosen own, per Shadowfax refusing all but Gandalf). | This should be the **rarest, latest-tier node** — flavor it as "a horse of legend" rather than a default freebie; only one such bonded mount at a time. |
| 3 | **Horn-calls that rally and terrify** — Helm Hammerhand's great horn "echoed in the Deep," causing "such fear on his enemies that they would flee." The tradition continues (Théoden/Éomer's horns at Helm's Deep, Rohan's charge at Pelennor). | **[Active] Horn of the Mark** — blow a horn item: nearby allied players/mounts get a temporary Speed+Strength "charge" buff, while nearby hostile mobs within a radius get a Fear-style effect (flee briefly / take a stack of Weakness), mirroring the historical rout Helm's horn caused. | Short duration, meaningful cooldown, and — thematically — **most effective when charging in formation on horseback** (bonus radius/duration when the caster themselves is mounted at time of use, weaker if used on foot). |
| 4 | **Endurance and hardiness of a plains people** — the Rohirrim are depicted as tough, weathered folk of the open Wold, used to long rides and exposure (general framing throughout *RotK*'s "The Ride of the Rohirrim" and "The Muster of Rohan"). | **[Passive] Wold-Hardened** — reduced hunger/exhaustion drain while traveling on horseback over long distances; bonus resistance to Slowness from difficult terrain (tall grass) while mounted. | Unlike Elves' tirelessness (which is unconditional), this bonus is **mount-conditional** — Rohirrim aren't personally tireless, their *horses* are what let them cover ground; dismounted, they tire like any Man. |
| 5 | **The charge/muster as a decisive tactic — "Ride now, ride now, ride to Gondor!"** — the Rohirrim's entire martial doctrine is the mass cavalry charge (the Ride of the Rohirrim breaking the siege of Minas Tirith, *RotK*, "The Ride of the Rohirrim" / "The Battle of the Pelennor Fields"). | **[Active, capstone-tier] Ride of the Rohirrim** — see capstone below. | — |
| 6 | **Rohan is plains-and-open-country folk — less at home in dense forest, mountains, or underground** (no equivalent Rohirric presence in Moria, Mirkwood, or similar closed terrain in the text; their whole identated geography is the open Wold/Riddermark). | **[Passive, drawback] Plains-Born** — a small movement-speed *penalty* (or `mining_efficiency`/no bonus at all, contrasted against Dwarf/Elf) while underground or in dense-canopy forest biomes without a mount — representing unfamiliarity/discomfort off the open plains. Mounted, this penalty is also naturally severe since horses can't easily navigate caves/dense forest at all (a horse-focused culture is *soft-countered* by terrain that excludes horses). | This is Rohan's core identity tradeoff: their power is *conditional and terrain-bound* in a way Elves (senses) and Gondor (discipline) are not. |

### 3.2 Rohan skill-tree branch sketch

**Tier 1 — Riders of the Mark (passive)**
1. *Horse-Lord* — mount speed/jump/fall-damage bonus + no combat penalty while mounted.
2. *Wold-Hardened* — reduced exhaustion while riding; resist grass/terrain slowdown while mounted.
3. *Plains-Born* (auto-granted drawback) — small penalty underground/deep forest, more severe when seeking to bring a mount there.

**Tier 2 — Riddermark Training (passive, req. Tier 1)**
4. *Mounted Swordsmanship* — bonus attack speed/no penalty attacking from horseback.
5. *Herdmaster* — tamed horses gain bonus max health & faster taming; horses you own take reduced damage from fall/environmental sources.
6. *Muster's Call* — bonus to nearby allied mounted players' speed when riding in a group (herd bonus, small radius aura).

**Tier 3 — Horn and Banner (mixed, req. 2 of Tier 2)**
7. *[Active] Horn of the Mark* — AoE ally Speed+Strength / enemy Fear-and-Weakness pulse, stronger while mounted.
8. *Éored Discipline* — passive: reduced fear/panic-effect duration for both rider and mount (won't flee from Wither Skeletons, Warped-fungus fear mobs, etc.).

**Tier 4 — Legends of Eorl's House (mixed, req. Tier 3)**
9. *Meara-Kinship* — bond a legendary-tier horse with enhanced stats immune to being spooked/stolen.
10. *Shieldmaiden's Resolve* — passive: fighting while dismounted no longer applies the Plains-Born speed penalty against a single target you're dueling (a nod to Éowyn standing alone against the Witch-king on foot after Snowmane falls).

**Capstone (Tier 5)**
- **"Ride of the Rohirrim"** — modeled directly on the Pelennor Fields charge (*"Forth Éorlingas!"*, *RotK*, "The Ride of the Rohirrim"). **[Active, long cooldown, mounted-only]**: for a short duration, the caster and all nearby mounted allied players get greatly increased movement speed, knockback on collision with hostile mobs (a "trample charge"), and full immunity to Fear/flee-effects; hostile mobs directly in the charge's path take a burst of trample damage. The single most build-defining Men capstone tied to positioning (must be mounted, must be moving) rather than a stationary channel like Gondor's.

**Sources:** Rohirrim horsemanship and horse-culture, Mearas lineage ([Tolkien Gateway, "Rohirrim"](https://tolkiengateway.net/wiki/Rohirrim), [Tolkien Gateway, "Mearas"](https://tolkiengateway.net/wiki/Mearas), [CanterCraze horse breed rundown](https://cantercraze.com/lord-of-the-rings-horses/)); Helm Hammerhand's horn and the rout it caused ([Henneth Annûn, "Horn of Helm Hammerhand"](http://www.henneth-annun.net/things_view.cfm?thid=263)); the Ride of the Rohirrim / Pelennor charge framing (*The Return of the King*, "The Ride of the Rohirrim," referenced via general chapter summaries and the film's "Forth Éorlingas" beat which closely tracks the book's charge).

## 4. DALE (Lake-men / Bowmen of the Long Lake)

### 4.1 Canonical traits → abilities

| # | Canonical trait & citation | Gameplay ability | Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Bard the Bowman, captain of the archer-company of Esgaroth, and the heirloom Black Arrow** — passed down for generations from Girion, Lord of Dale, through to Bard, who uses the last, hidden Black Arrow (guided by the thrush's warning of Smaug's bare patch) to slay the dragon (*The Hobbit*, "Fire and Water"). | **[Active] Black Arrow** — a craftable/rare special arrow (limited uses, like a charge item) that, when fired from a fully-drawn bow, deals bonus "armor-piercing" damage that ignores a portion of target armor/mob armor-toughness — modeling the one arrow strong enough to punch a dragon's belly. | Very limited supply (crafted rarely, or found, not spammable) — it is *the* heirloom-tier signature item, not a repeatable spam ability. |
| 2 | **Dale/Lake-town's archer-militia culture** — Bard commands "a company of archers" defending Esgaroth against Smaug; the whole town musters bowmen at need (*The Hobbit*, "Fire and Water"). | **[Passive] Bowman-Militia** — faster bow-draw speed (similar family to Elven Marksman but themed as trained-militia rather than innate elvish skill) and improved accuracy (reduced arrow spread) specifically when firing at range >10 blocks, echoing volley-fire discipline. | Dale's archery bonus is **trained, not innate** — unlike Elves, Dale gets no passive perception/sight bonus to go with it; they must actually spot targets themselves (no bonus render distance/highlighting). |
| 3 | **Dale reborn as a trade city, "Men of the Lake and Men of Dale... friendship of Men, Elves and Dwarves"** — after Smaug's fall, Dale and Esgaroth are rebuilt on trade between Erebor, the Woodland Realm, and the wider world (*The Hobbit*, "The Return Journey" / Appendix A framing of Dale's re-founding). | **[Passive] Merchant of the Long Lake** — better trade prices/emeralds from villager or faction traders; a small XP/loot bonus from fishing (Lake-town's economy is explicitly lake-and-trade based). | Purely economic/utility — Dale gets **no combat bonus** from this trait, reinforcing that their martial identity is narrowly archery, not an all-round warrior culture like Gondor or Rohan. |

### 4.2 Dale skill-tree branch sketch

**Tier 1 — Folk of the Lake (passive)**
1. *Bowman-Militia* — faster draw, tighter arrow spread at range.
2. *Merchant of the Long Lake* — better trade prices, fishing loot bonus.
3. *Wood-and-Water Town* (auto-granted drawback) — increased fire vulnerability.

**Tier 2 — Archer's Discipline (passive, req. Tier 1)**
4. *Volley Fire* — bonus accuracy/damage the more allied Dale archers are firing in the same area (militia-volley synergy, small aura-based stacking buff).
5. *Watchful Eye* — early warning indicator for boss-tier/named hostiles in a modest radius.
6. *Bowyer's Craft* — crafting bonus: bows and arrows cost less material / higher durability when crafted by a Dale player.

**Tier 3 — Heirs of Girion (mixed, req. 2 of Tier 2)**
7. *[Active] Black Arrow* — rare craftable armor-piercing shot.
8. *Steady Hand* — passive: no accuracy loss firing while moving or from a boat (lake-culture mobility).

**Tier 4 — Captains of Esgaroth (mixed)**
9. *Town-Militia Muster* — [Active] short-duration party buff: nearby allied players get bonus arrow damage and draw-speed (Bard rallying the town's archers).
10. *Dragon-Scale Lore* — passive: bonus damage specifically vs. boss-tier/large mobs (Ender Dragon, Wither, mod bosses) representing dragon-slaying heritage.

**Capstone (Tier 5)**
- **"The Last Arrow" / "Slayer of the Worm"** — modeled directly on Bard's kill-shot on Smaug (*The Hobbit*, "Fire and Water": guided by the thrush, aiming for the bare patch on the dragon's breast). **[Active, single/rare charge, high cooldown or consumes the Black Arrow item]**: a single fully-charged shot that, if it lands on a boss-tier or sufficiently large hostile entity, deals a massive bonus-damage critical strike that ignores a large portion of armor/toughness — echoing "one arrow still remained… he drew it to the barb" — the highest-risk, highest-reward capstone of the four cultures (all-in on a single well-aimed shot rather than an AoE/party buff).

## 5. Cross-culture design notes (for balance & flavor coherence)

- **Elves** are the "always-on senses & survival" culture — nearly all their nodes are passive, environment-agnostic buffs (sight, cold, tirelessness), paid for by one slow-burn narrative debuff (Sea-Longing) and one situational one (Deep-Dark Unease). This matches the base study's finding that Elf is already "one of the strongest all-round picks" with no player-facing drawback — the tree should *lean into* that by keeping Elf power passive/ambient, while giving Men their power through **active, cooldown-gated, situational** abilities (banners, horns, arrows, channels) so Men remain competitive through skill/timing rather than raw stat parity.
- **Gondor** = the support/leadership culture (healing, rallying, banners) — powerful but conditional on allies being nearby and on the (optional) honor-behavior gate.
- **Rohan** = the mount-dependent burst-mobility/shock-cavalry culture — extremely strong charging on horseback, mediocre-to-penalized on foot or off-plains, the most terrain-conditional of the four.
- **Dale** = the glass-cannon precision-archer/economy culture — narrow but sharp (single-target burst via Black Arrow/capstone), weak generalist combat, and a clean elemental weakness (fire) tying directly to their town's history.
- Together the three Men cultures deliberately do **not** overlap in their signature active: Gondor heals/rallies, Rohan charges, Dale snipes — giving the "Men" side of the roster three genuinely distinct fantasies despite sharing a mortal, non-magical baseline (per the brief's note that Men have no innate magical resistances — none of the Men trees grant Ring-resistance or fear-immunity as strong/unconditional as the Elf's Unbowed Will; Gondor's fear-resistance nodes are explicitly weaker/conditional, and Rohan's is mount-bound).
