# The Blood of the Peoples — A Field Guide to Racial Traits

Every people of Middle-earth is born into its own nature. The moment you take up a race in the
Player's Book, its innate gifts and burdens settle onto you — no points spent, nothing to unlock.
This is who you *are*, before you ever learn a single skill.

Read the tables below like a character sheet. A few things worth knowing first:

- **✦** marks a boon, **✖** a burden. Most peoples carry both.
- **Hearts** are what you'd expect: +3 hearts means six extra points of health on the bar.
- **Percentages** scale the base value — "+15% attack speed" means you simply swing faster.
- **Potion-style effects** (Night Vision, Strength, Weakness, Regeneration, and so on) show up as a
  familiar icon in your inventory, exactly like a brewed potion would.
- Some gifts sleep until the world calls them out. These are marked *(in italics)* with their
  trigger:
  - *starlight* — night, under open sky
  - *underground* — no open sky overhead
  - *in darkness* — night **or** underground
  - *in daylight* — daytime under direct open sky
  - *near death* — at or below 35% health
  - *in the deep dark* — a lightless place with no sky above

All numbers are set in the server config and can be retuned with `/kindreds config`.

## The Firstborn — Elves

Keen, tireless, and quicker than the eye. Elves fight with speed and precision rather than brute
weight, and they mend from wounds that would put a Man in the ground. The one place they falter is
the one place a Dwarf feels most at home: the black, unlit deep.

| Trait | | Effect |
|---|:--:|---|
| Star-sight | ✦ | Permanent **Night Vision** |
| Elven-swift | ✦ | **+12%** movement speed |
| Deft hands | ✦ | **+15%** attack speed |
| Woodland-quiet | ✦ | Mobs notice you late (detection range **−4**) |
| Light-footed | ✦ | Safe fall distance **+8** blocks |
| Hard to hurt | ✦ | **+1 heart**, and heals from wounds that would fell a Man |
| Unwearied | ✦ | Never goes hungry; needs no sleep |
| Starlit Grace *(starlight)* | ✦ | **Regeneration + Speed** beneath the open night sky |
| Never sickens | ✦ | Immune to **Hunger** and **Nausea**; cold cannot freeze you |
| Unstained by the Shadow | ✦ | Curses, poison and other ills **wear off roughly 3× faster** |
| Unease in the deep dark *(deep dark)* | ✖ | **Weakness** in lightless underground places |

## Durin's Folk — Dwarves

The anvil of Middle-earth. Dwarves shrug off the flame that slew dragons, endure blows that break
lesser folk, and come alive in the deep stone. Slow above ground, unbreakable below it, and the
hardest of all peoples to corrupt.

| Trait | | Effect |
|---|:--:|---|
| Fire-hardy | ✦ | Burns **60% briefer** — resistant to fire, though not immune |
| Stone-hardy | ✦ | **+3 hearts** |
| Unmovable | ✦ | **+0.5** knockback resistance |
| Delver | ✦ | **+0.5** mining efficiency (breaks stone faster) |
| Deep-Delver *(underground)* | ✦ | **Haste + Resistance** while no sky is above you |
| Unbowed by the Shadow | ✦ | The most resistant of all — ills **wear off ~3× faster** |
| Iron constitution | ✦ | Hunger drains **noticeably slower** |
| Short-legged | ✖ | **−10%** movement speed |

## The Secondborn — Men

Jacks of every trade and masters of their own fate. Men aren't the strongest or the swiftest, but
they're hardy, tall, at home on any road, and dangerous precisely when you think they're beaten. The
price of their freedom is that no ancient gift shields them from anything.

| Trait | | Effect |
|---|:--:|---|
| Vigour of the Edain | ✦ | **+2 hearts** |
| Valour | ✦ | **+10%** melee damage |
| Tall of Númenor | ✦ | **+0.5** reach with blocks **and** in combat |
| Wayfarer | ✦ | No slowdown on soul-sand/honey/mud, **no sliding on ice**, **+4%** speed |
| Last Stand *(near death)* | ✦ | **Strength + Resistance** while gravely wounded |
| The Doom of Men | ✖ | **No innate resistances** — fire, dark and dread all bite in full |
| Mortal weariness | ✖ | Grows tired after long without sleep |

## The Little Folk — Hobbits

Underestimate them at your peril. Hobbits are almost impossible to notice, unnaturally lucky, and
harder to corrupt than anyone in Middle-earth — the Ring itself struggled to find a grip. They're no
brawlers, but a well-fed Hobbit is a stubborn thing to kill.

| Trait | | Effect |
|---|:--:|---|
| Unseen | ✦ | Mobs notice you very late (detection range **−8**) |
| Luck of the Shire | ✦ | **+3** luck (better loot and fishing) |
| Nimble | ✦ | **+0.5** sneaking speed |
| Sure-footed | ✦ | Safe fall distance **+3** blocks |
| Resilient of heart | ✦ | Evil finds no purchase — ills **wear off ~3× faster** |
| Well-fed *(belly full)* | ✦ | While food is **18+**, slowly **regenerates** health |
| Little folk | ✖ | **−1 heart** |
| No warrior's arm | ✖ | **−20%** melee damage |
| Tires | ✖ | Grows weary after long without sleep |

## Mordor's Soldiery — Orcs

Bred for slaughter and born to the dark. Orcs hit harder than Men, see in the black, and grow
stronger the deeper the night — but drag one into open sun and it wilts. They'll eat anything, and I
mean anything, without so much as a stomach-ache.

| Trait | | Effect |
|---|:--:|---|
| Eyes of the dark | ✦ | Permanent **Night Vision** |
| Bred for war | ✦ | **+15%** melee damage |
| Soldier's hide | ✦ | **+1 heart** |
| Children of the Dark *(in darkness)* | ✦ | **Strength** by night or underground |
| Iron guts | ✦ | Immune to the **Hunger** from rotten flesh and foul food |
| Dread of the Sun *(in daylight)* | ✖ | **Weakness II + Slowness** under open sun |
| Tires | ✖ | Grows weary after long without sleep |

The lowest of the low, driven by the lash. What a Snaga lacks in strength it makes up in speed and

## The Uruk-hai

Saruman's masterwork — orcs that don't fear the daylight. Big, brutal, and tireless, the fighting
Uruk grows *bolder* in the open sun where a common orc would cower. It hits like a landslide; it just
doesn't hit *quickly*.

| Trait | | Effect |
|---|:--:|---|
| Fighting-Uruk | ✦ | **+3 hearts** |
| Crushing blow | ✦ | **+20%** melee damage (strongest of the Orc-kind) |
| Braced | ✦ | **+0.3** knockback resistance |
| Quick march | ✦ | **+5%** movement speed |
| Sun-defiant *(in daylight)* | ✦ | **Strength** under open sun — bold where lesser orcs falter |
| Tireless | ✦ | Hunger drains slower; weariness sets in far later than for others |
| Iron guts | ✦ | Immune to **Hunger** from foul food |
| Heavy-handed | ✖ | **−15%** attack speed |

## At a glance

| People | Where they shine | Where they break |
|---|---|---|
| Elf | Speed, quick strikes, stealth, sight, healing, Shadow-proof | Weakens in the lightless deep |
| Dwarf | Fire, +3 hearts, might underground, near-uncorruptible | Slow above ground |
| Man | Versatility, +2 hearts, reach, Wayfarer, Last Stand | No resistances at all |
| Hobbit | Stealth, luck, Ring-resistance, well-fed healing | Frail, poor in a fight |
| Orc | Damage, night-sight, dark-strength, iron guts | Dread of the Sun |
| Goblin | Climbing, stealth, dark-strength | Dread of the Sun, frail |
| Snaga | Speed, stealth, dark-strength | Dread of the Sun, weakest |
| Uruk-hai | +3 hearts, huge damage, bold in daylight, tireless | Slow to swing |

*A word to the wise: play to your blood. An Orc who fights at noon is a dead Orc; a Dwarf who tries
to out-run trouble will be caught. The people you choose isn't a coat of paint — it's how you'll have
to live.*
